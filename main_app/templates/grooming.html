Pet grooming tips and tricks





Have you ever seen your dog roll about on the floor, lick her coat, or chew her fur? These are the methods she use to keep herself clean. However, she may want some assistance from you in order to look and smell her best. But don't worry, we'll be here to assist you. Continue reading to learn how to maintain your dog's fur, skin, nails, teeth, ears, and paws clean and healthy.


DO Help Your Pet Feel Comfortable Around Grooming Tools

Tangles and mats are a big source of anxiety for pet owners, especially those who have long-haired pets. Tangles and mats can irritate your pet's skin, so combing his or her coat on a daily basis is the best way to avoid them.
A simple brush can be frightening to certain animals. Rebecca Lohnes, the Behavior & Training Manager at Lollypop Farm, suggests starting carefully, especially if your pet is nervous. "If your pet is afraid of brushes, using a high-value food like peanut butter or cream cheese is a fantastic way to expose them to one," Rebecca explained. "You may begin by providing the goodie when the brush is in close proximity to them. Work up to using the brush with them, starting with brief sessions and gradually increasing the time until they are calm and comfortable."


DON’T Use Products Intended For Human Usage On Your Pet

While your personal shampoo may smell nicer than your dog's, these products frequently include chemicals that might irritate your pet's skin or cause harm to their coat. Human shampoo lacks the necessary pH balance for your dog's fur, and using it might result in an unexpected trip to the veterinarian.


DO Make Bathtime Stress Free

Make a strategy for how to approach bathtime in a stress-free manner when a bath is required due to scents or a mud hole your dog discovered outside. Bathing your pet with a partner may be beneficial since one person can focus on your pet's safety and praising excellent behavior while the other does the washing, massaging, and rinsing.


DON’T Overlook Simple Nail Maintenance Techniques

Your dog's nails are definitely becoming a little long if you don't take him to the groomer on a regular basis. While some dogs are OK with their owners touching their paws and trimming their nails, others may be wary. Another way to keep your nails in good shape is to sand them on a baking sheet. Simply place the sandpaper on top of the sheet, and when your pet places their paw on it, give them with a treat.


DO Call Your Vet If You Have Any Concerns

Your veterinarian is always your best resource, now more than ever, if your pet has a health-related concern. If you detect any problems with your pet's health or behavior, the first place you should go is to your local veterinarian, who can help you and determine whether your pet need emergency medical assistance.


Supplies for grooming pets

Here are some grooming tools you could require:

•Shampoo that is suited for your pet's age and species (kittens and puppies need gentle shampoo; very young animals need products free of harsh pesticides; and ferrets should have ferret shampoo)
•To get a great lather, use a large cup or a small bucket filled with water.
•Cotton swabs
•Cleaner for the ears
•Controlling parasites products (ask your veterinarian about what is needed in your area for fleas, ticks and mites)
•Comb made of metal
•Using a brush (there are many styles to choose from: pin, rake, slicker, mitt or curry)
•Nail trimmers (find out what size works best for your pet's nails)
•File with a nail (some animals will actually sleep while their people file each toenail)
•Powder for styptic (to use if you accidentally cut a nail too short)
•An ophthalmic ointment is a type of ointment that is used to (used in the eyes to protect them from shampoo and debris)
•Conditioner or detangler (great for combing through long hair before a final rinse)
•Attachment for your shower spray (very helpful for rinsing your pet)
•A blow dryer (because some animals can chill easily, but be careful not to overheat the pet)
•Toothbrush and toothpaste for animals
•Security scissors for hair trimming
•Snapper (if you want to learn to style your pet)

in addition:

•Use ceramic blades when trimming many animals at the same time. With repeated usage, they remain cold and sharp. As you clip, it's a good practice to touch the clipper blade to the back of your wrist. It's fine to keep using it if it's cool enough to touch your wrist. Spray with Kool Lube and recheck on your wrist if it's too hot.
•A grooming table is a terrific method to keep your dog or cat in position while also preventing back strain. For footing, they have a rubber mat surface.
•If you don't have a grooming table, use a rubber mat on the floor to aid with footing. This will motivate you to stand instead of lying down.


In summary, here are 8 do-it-yourself pet grooming techniques


•Stay calm and bring on the treats.
•Brush your pet regularly.
•Stay on top of hair mats.
•Schedule regular baths.
•Choose a shampoo made for pets.
•Don’t go for the tomato juice when skunked.
•Trim nails regularly.
•Do not rush.
